    tab trigger: negative

    Equations that include negative numbers require the use of an image with an alt tag to ensure the screen reader accurately interprets the negative sign.The negative symbol is written as a standard hyphen.

    <img width="3" height="3" src="null.png" alt="negative" />-10 produces -10 which the screen reader speaks as "negative ten."

    Note the difference between the negative sign ("negative") and minus sign (&minus;) in the following equation:

    -10x − 7y = -6z

    tab trigger: functionof

    Similarly, in lessons dealing with functions, the of in f of x or function of a number, the implied of is communicated to screen readers through a null image with an alt tag.

    <em>f</em><img width="3" height="3" src="images/null.png" alt="of" />(<em>x</em>) produces f(x) which the screen reader speaks as "f of x."

    Properly formatted, the function notation appears as f(x)

    Equation formatting should only be applied to actual equations including their variables. If plain text lesson content comes before or after, do not include that in the <span>

    For example, math lessons often reference formulas such as f(x) = 19x2 − 2y. Increasing the font size and differentiating the font-family helps students scan for these crucial pieces of information quickly as they work and review.
